I am a postdoc researcher in the System and Networking Group at Microsoft Research Cambridge, UK. My research focus on sovling the physical layer challenges of designing an all optical switch for next generation data center. I love the multidisciplinary enviroment at Microsft. On one hand, I could contribute my previous reserach experience in photonic devices and optical communicaiton systems to the projects. On the other hand, I could gain a lot of knowlege from colleages who works on networking and application layers in the cloud.

Before joining Microsoft Reserach, I worked as a Reserach Associate at the Opitcal Network Group at univeristy colleage London, UK for the COMIMO project (2012 - 2017). In this project I built a 10×10 mode division multiplexing system over a 24km ring core fiber in collaboration with Univeristy of Southampton and University of Central Florida with digital signal processing algorithms that I developed to enable fast convergence rate and low complexity for the optical mulitple-input and multiple-ouput (MIMO) system. We manged to achieve a 1.12 terabit per second tranmission rate over an single optical fiber.

I obainted my PhD from Dublin City Univeristy, Ireland, where I worked on optical burst switching using various fast wavelength tunable semicondutor lasers.
